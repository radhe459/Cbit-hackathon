Punishment for Ragging

  • If any student is caught ragging another student, he or she can be punished. Additionally, the punishment can be in the form of:
  • Administrative actions against the student :
    -For example, suspension from the college, suspension from the hostel, etc.
  • Police complaint against the student :
    • -If a complaint or FIR is filed against the student, then a case can be made under the Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC) which will lead to more serious charges, like jail time or a fine.

Administrative Punishments

  • If you are caught ragging, the Anti-Ragging Committee can punish you with:

  1. Suspension from attending classes and academic privileges.

  2. Withholding/withdrawing scholarship/fellowship and other benefits.

  3. Debarring you from appearing in any test/examination.

  4. Withholding results.

  5. Debarring you from representing the college in any regional, national or international meet, tournament, youth festival, etc. Suspension/expulsion from the hostel.

  6. Rustication from the college for a period ranging from one to four semesters.

Police Complaint

In more serious cases of ragging, if an FIR or police complaint is filed, the student may be punished under the Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC).

Physical Abuse

Ragging may include subjecting a student to physical abuse and violence.

  • If a person intentionally hurts someone, he can be punished with jail time of up to 1 year and/or a fine up to Rs. 10000.
    -For example, punching a student or kicking a student.
  • If someone intentionally causes someone grievous hurt, he can be punished with jail time of up to 7 years and a fine.
    -For example, beating up a student which results in fractures or injuries.

Sexual Abuse

  • If someone sexually abuses a student while ragging them, it is an offence.
    -For example, if someone touches a woman sexually and forcefully, or harasses her, or stalks her, it is an offence.

Restricting Movement

  • Ragging may include restricting the movement of a student or locking him up.

  • It is an offence if someone voluntarily obstructs a person so he is not able to move in a certain direction.
    -For example, not letting a student come to the college canteen by obstructing his way. The punishment for this is jail time of up to 1 month and/or a fine of Rs. 5000.
